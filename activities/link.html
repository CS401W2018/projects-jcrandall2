What goes in the Header?

The header typically contains the website's logo, title, and tagline. It may also include a navigation menu, search bar, or introductory content that represents the website.

What goes in the Navigation (Nav)?

The navigation section (Nav) contains links to various parts of the website, such as Home, About, Services, or Contact pages. These links help users navigate the site efficiently. While Nav can go in the header, it is not mandatory. Nav can also appear in other parts of the page, like a sidebar or footer.

Nav can also contain external links, like a link to an HTML validator or other resources.

What goes in the Main?

The Main section is where the primary content of the page resides. This can include articles, blogs, product listings, or other central information relevant to the purpose of the page.

What goes in the Footer?

The footer typically contains copyright information, contact details, social media links, and other relevant information about the website or organization. It is the last section of the page and often includes links to other parts of the site or external resources.
